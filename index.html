💉 Insulin Calculator

Calculate bolus dose and your personal Insulin Sensitivity Factor (ISF) & Insulin-to-Carb Ratio (ICR).

📐 Step 1 — Find Your Ratios (from Total Daily Dose)

The total units of insulin you inject in a full day — add basal (background) + all bolus (meal) doses together.

ISF (1800 Rule) — mg/dL per unit How many mg/dL one unit of insulin drops your blood sugar. Formula: 1800 ÷ Total Daily Dose.
ICR (500 Rule) — g carbs per unit How many grams of carbs one unit of insulin covers. Formula: 500 ÷ Total Daily Dose.
💊 Step 2 — Calculate Your Dose

Your blood glucose reading right now, before the meal.

The blood sugar level you want to reach after dosing. Typically 80–100 mg/dL.

Total grams of carbohydrates in the meal you are about to eat.

How many grams of carbs one unit of insulin covers. Use the ICR from Step 1, or enter your prescribed ratio.

Insulin Sensitivity Factor — how much one unit of insulin lowers your blood sugar (in mg/dL). Use the ISF from Step 1, or your doctor's prescribed value. Used to calculate the correction dose when your current BG is above target.

⚠️ Always confirm doses with your diabetes care team.
